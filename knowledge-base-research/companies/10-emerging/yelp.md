# Yelp - Business Discovery and Reviews Platform

## Executive Summary

**Company**: Yelp Inc.
**Founded**: October 2004
**Founders**: Jeremy Stoppelman (CEO), Russel Simmons, David Galbraith
**Headquarters**: 350 Mission Street, San Francisco, California, U.S.
**Status**: Public Company (NYSE: YELP) - IPO March 2012
**Revenue**: $1.45B USD (TTM 2024-2025), up from $1.41B (2024) and $1.33B (2023)
**Market Cap**: $2.11B (2025)
**Employees**: ~5,116 (2024)
**Funding**: $56M across 6 rounds (2004-IPO)

**Core Mission**: Connect people with great local businesses through user-generated reviews, discovery tools, and reservation systems.

---

## 1. Company Overview & History

### Origins
- **2004**: Founded by PayPal alumni Jeremy Stoppelman (VP of Engineering at PayPal) and Russel Simmons
- **Initial Concept**: Started as an email-based service where people would ask friends for recommendations
- **Pivot**: Founders shifted to a public review platform where users could share experiences with local businesses openly
- **2012**: Became publicly traded on NYSE (ticker: YELP) on March 2, 2012

### Key Acquisitions
- **2014**: Acquired Restaurant-Kritik (Germany) with 330,000+ reviews of 94,000+ restaurants to expand European presence
- **2014**: Acquired Qype (German review site) - though half the database was reportedly lost
- **2014**: Acquired CityVox (France) for European expansion
- **2016**: Acquired SeatMe (restaurant reservation platform)
- **2016**: Acquired NoWait (waitlist management service) - combined to create Yelp Guest Manager

### Recent Developments
- **2016**: Announced drastic scaling back of operations outside North America, terminating international employees across 30+ markets
- **2021**: Relocated corporate headquarters to smaller space (subleased from Salesforce)
- **2023**: Closed offices in Phoenix, Arizona and Hamburg, Germany (less than 6% office utilization)
- **2024-2025**: Massive AI transformation with 35+ new AI features announced

## 2. Core Platform Features

### 2.1 Consumer Discovery Features (2025 AI-Powered)

#### Yelp Assistant (AI Chatbot)
- **Launch**: Major expansion in 2025
- **Capabilities**: Answers any question about restaurants, bars, local attractions, retailers
- **Growth**: 400% year-over-year increase in project submissions (Q2 2025)
- **Future**: Will become primary discovery and transaction interface in 2026
- **Integration**: Merges instant answers, booking, and customer messaging in one interface

#### Menu Vision
- **Launch**: October 21, 2025 (iOS & Android)
- **Technology**: AI + Augmented Reality + Text Recognition
- **Features**: Point phone camera at menus to view:
  - Food, drink, and dessert photos
  - Review excerpts about popular dishes
  - Real-time menu item processing

#### Natural Language & Voice Search
- **Powered by**: AI/LLMs
- **Functionality**: Search using conversational language
- **Results**: Smarter, more relevant results and suggestions

#### Popular Offerings
- **Technology**: Large Language Models (LLMs)
- **Purpose**: Highlight most mentioned items, services, or experiences
- **Benefit**: Quick visual snapshot of what customers love most

#### Review Insights
- **Technology**: LLMs analyzing reviewer sentiment
- **Categories Analyzed**:
  - Customer experience
  - Pricing
  - Facilities
  - Job quality
- **Display**: Scores shown above reviews for quick information access

### 2.2 Review System

#### Review Quality & Filtering
- **Recommended Reviews**: Most reliable and helpful reviews (shown on main profile)
- **Not Recommended Reviews**: ~25% of reviews flagged as potentially fake, biased, or unhelpful
- **Review Length**: Yelp reviews are significantly longer than competitors
  - Only 2% of Yelp reviews <100 characters
  - Compared to 50% of Google reviews <100 characters (FTC research)

#### Review Impact
- **Harvard Business School Study**: 1-star improvement = 5-7% revenue increase for restaurants
- **Consumer Behavior**: 82% of Yelp users hire or buy from a business within a week of searching
- **Platform Usage**: 2.5 million daily visitors
- **Market Position**: 2nd most popular review site (after Google)

### 2.3 Reservation & Waitlist System

#### Yelp Guest Manager
**Overview**: Comprehensive front-of-house management solution integrating:
- Yelp Waitlist
- Reservations
- Kiosk
- Takeout
- Table Management
- POS integrations

**Key Features**:
- **Digital Reservations & Waitlist**: Integrated buttons on Yelp pages and websites
- **Multi-Platform Distribution**: Yelp, Google, Google Maps, Apple Maps, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook
- **AI Wait-Time Predictions**: Up to 92-96% accuracy
- **Table-Specific Reservations**: Guaranteed seating areas (2025 feature)
- **Waitlist Status**: Real-time updates on iPhone lock screen/home screen
- **Credit Card Holds**: Reduce no-shows
- **Two-Way Texting**: Seamless guest communication
- **Custom Floor Maps**: For hosts and servers
- **POS Integration**: Automatic table status updates

#### Yelp Host (AI Voice Assistant)
- **Availability**: 24/7 automated call handling
- **Capabilities**:
  - Handle reservations and waitlist
  - Screen spam and wrong numbers
  - Answer common questions
  - Sync with real-time table availability
- **Pricing**: $149/month ($99/month for Guest Manager customers)

#### Yelp Receptionist
- **Target**: Non-restaurant local businesses
- **Features**:
  - Capture project details
  - Vet leads
  - Deliver call recordings, transcripts, summaries to Yelp for Business inbox
- **Pricing**: $99/month

**Guest Manager Statistics**:
- 433 million diners booked tables since 2017
- 8x more consumers reached than OpenTable
- Average savings: $163/month vs. OpenTable
- **Pricing**: $99-$299/month flat rate (no setup fee, no cover fees)

### 2.4 DoorDash Partnership
- **Launch**: Recent (2024-2025)
- **Integration**: Powers ordering and delivery on Yelp
- **Reach**: 500,000+ merchants with takeout/delivery options

## 5. Yelp Fusion API (Developer Platform)

### API Overview
- **Name**: Yelp Places API (formerly Yelp Fusion API)
- **Access**: Self-service public API
- **Base URL**: https://api.yelp.com/v3
- **Documentation**: https://docs.developer.yelp.com/

### API Capabilities
**Business Search**:
- Search by keyword, category, location
- Filter by price level, phone number
- Rich business data retrieval:
  - Name, address, phone
  - Photos
  - Yelp ratings
  - Price levels
  - Hours of operation

### API Types
1. **Public Yelp Places API**: Self-serve, accessible via Yelp Account
2. **Yelp Partner APIs**: Additional features and data (requires partnership)
3. **GraphQL Option**: Alternative query method for parts of Yelp Places data

### Authentication
- **Method**: Private API Keys
- **OAuth**: Authorization Code Workflow with refresh tokens (for partner APIs)
- **Setup**: Create app to obtain API key

### Pricing Tiers
1. **Trial Plan**: 5,000 free API calls for 30 days (evaluation only, not for commercial deployment)
2. **Starter Plan**: Details in pricing documentation
3. **Plus Plan**: Details in pricing documentation
4. **Enterprise Plan**: Details in pricing documentation

**Rate Limiting**:
- Limits reset at midnight UTC
- Exceeding limits returns 429 status code (Too Many Requests)

## 6. Advertising & Monetization

### Revenue Breakdown
**Primary Revenue: Advertising (90%+)**
- Local advertising: 76.8% of total revenue
- Ad resales & partnerships: Additional revenue
- Total advertising: $907M (2018 annual report)

**Secondary Revenue (<10%)**:
- Transaction fees: $13.6M (2018)
- Subscription services (Guest Manager, etc.): Part of "other services" ($21.5M in 2018)
- Data licensing: Enterprise customers for local content/business intelligence

### Advertising Model

#### Cost-Per-Click (CPC) Pricing
- **Range**: $0.30 - $40 per click
  - Low: $0.30 (low-interest categories, e.g., sushi in Texas)
  - High: $40 (high-value services, e.g., legal services in competitive markets)
- **Minimum Daily Spend**: $5/day average
- **Typical Monthly Budgets**:
  - Small businesses: $300-$500/month
  - Mid-range advertisers: $1,000-$2,500/month
  - High-budget advertisers: $5,000+/month

#### Advertising Products

**1. Yelp Ads (Sponsored Listings)**
- Placement: "Sponsored Results" sections on search pages
- Competitor Page Display: Ads shown on competitor business pages
- CPC Model: Pay only when users click

**2. Premium Profiles**
- **Branded Profiles**: Video uploads, slideshows, multiple CTAs
- **Enhanced Profiles**: Branded profile + hide rival ads

**3. Yelp Ads Certified Partners Program (2018)**
- Allows collaborative agencies to manage/sell campaigns on behalf of business clients
- Ad resale revenue stream

### Transaction Revenue

**Yelp Deals**:
- Prepaid vouchers for shopping at businesses
- Commission received only when voucher is redeemed

**Gift Certificates**: Commission-based revenue

**Food Delivery Integration**: Grubhub/Eat24/DoorDash commission sharing

## 7. German Market Presence

### History & Challenges
**Expansion Efforts**:
- **2014**: Acquired Restaurant-Kritik (330,000+ reviews, 94,000+ restaurants across Germany)
- **2014**: Acquired Qype (German review site) - half database reportedly lost
- **2016**: Halted international expansion, terminated international employees across 30+ markets
- **2023**: Closed Hamburg, Germany office (less than 6% utilization)

### Current Status in Germany
- **Market Position**: "Sort of used in Germany, but not a whole lot" (user reports)
- **Competitor Dominance**: TripAdvisor more popular for restaurant reviews
- **Active Listings**: Still maintains restaurant listings in Berlin, Hamburg, and other German cities
- **Language Support**: Available in both German and English

### Market Analysis
- **Challenge**: Limited adoption compared to North American markets
- **Competition**: Strong local and European competitors (TripAdvisor, Google Reviews)
- **Strategy**: Minimal investment post-2016 international pullback
- **Opportunity**: German market underserved by Yelp's advanced AI features

## 10. Key Takeaways for WiesbadenAfterDark

### Features to Adopt
1. AI-powered conversational search and voice search
2. Review insights with sentiment analysis using LLMs
3. Comprehensive business owner dashboard with competitor analytics
4. Multi-platform reservation integration (Google Maps, Apple Maps, social media)
5. AI-powered automated messaging for customer inquiries
6. Real-time waitlist/entry list management with SMS notifications
7. CPC advertising model for sustainable monetization
8. Photo/media management with smart selection algorithms
9. Professional review response tools (public + private)

1. Overly broad business categories (stay nightlife-focused)
2. Aggressive international expansion (focus on German market first)

4. Complex, expensive pricing tiers (keep accessible to small promoters)

### Competitive Advantages
1. Niche focus on nightlife vs. Yelp's general local business model
2. European/German market expertise vs. Yelp's failed expansion
3. Event-centric vs. business-centric platform design
4. Social-first community building vs. review-only focus
5. Real-time nightlife data vs. static business information
6. Music-driven discovery vs. generic category search
7. Verified attendee reviews vs. 25% "Not Recommended" reviews
8. Collaborative ecosystem vs. competitive advertising model
9. Privacy-conscious design vs. heavy data collection
10. Authentic vibe matching vs. generic recommendations
