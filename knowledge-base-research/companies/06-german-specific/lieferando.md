# Lieferando Research

## Basic Info
- **Website:** lieferando.de
- **Founded:** 2009
- **Founders:** Jörg Gerbig, Jitse Groen, Kai Hansen, Christoph Gerber
- **HQ:** Berlin, Germany
- **Parent Company:** Just Eat Takeaway.com (acquired 2014 by Takeaway.com, merged with Just Eat in 2020)
  - In February 2025, Prosus announced acquisition of Just Eat Takeaway.com for €4.1 billion (completed October 2025)
- **Target Market:** Germany and Austria (operates under Lieferando brand)
- **Market Position:** #1 food delivery platform in Germany with 75% usage rate (2024)
- **Restaurant Partners:** 35,000+ restaurants
- **Market Share:** ~40% dominance after Lieferando-Lieferheld merger (2019)
- **Global Reach:** Part of Just Eat Takeaway operating in 17 countries

## Product Features

### Ordering Flow:
- **Address Entry:** Save multiple addresses, use postcode/street name, or auto-location
- **Restaurant Discovery:** Browse by cuisine, "Loved in your area" recommendations
- **Menu Browsing:** Enhanced with bigger menu images, allergen information
- **Quick Re-ordering:** One-tap reorder of previous meals
- **Order Timeframe:** Typically 30-45 minutes delivery
- **Pickup Option:** Available for self-collection

### Payment Methods:
- PayPal
- Klarna
- Credit Card
- Apple Pay
- Cash on delivery (if restaurant allows)
- SSL encryption for payment protection
- **Fixed delivery fee** model

### Delivery Tracking:
- **Food Tracker®:** Real-time tracking from kitchen to doorstep
- Push notifications for order status updates
- Live order progress visibility

### Mobile App Features:
- **iOS & Android:** Available on both platforms
- **Personal Account:**
  - Multiple address storage
  - Order history
  - Favorite restaurants
  - Loyalty stamps and points collection
- **Performance:** 13 visits per unique visitor on average
- **Monthly Active Users:** 10+ million (stable Q1-Q2 2024)
- **App Store Rankings:** #1 in Restaurants and Delivery category (Germany)

### API & Integration:
- **Official API:** Just Eat Takeaway Public Developers Portal
- **POS Integration:** Two-way interface available
  - Default: POS API excludes tablet usage (no simultaneous use)
  - Hybrid Mode: Orders accepted on tablet, then sent to POS (Scoober delivery only)
- **Third-Party Integrations:**
  - HubRise (via Just Eat Takeaway Bridge)
  - MERGEPORT (DE, AT, CH, NL, BE, FR, LU, DK, BG, PL, CZ)
  - Primasello (Cloud Portal)
  - SambaPOS
- **GitHub Spec:** Unofficial API specification available

## Restaurant Partner Features

### Partner Hub Dashboard:
- **Central Control:** Manage all daily business operations
- **Opening Hours Management:**
  - Set/change regular hours
  - Update for public holidays
  - Mark temporary closures
- **Menu Management:**
  - Add/edit items with descriptions and images
  - Mark items as unavailable (sold-out)
  - Allergen information validation
  - **Pro Tip:** Menu items with images get 4x more orders
- **Analytics Section:**
  - Key performance figures
  - Real-time insights
  - Business performance tracking
- **Account Management:**
  - Business information updates
  - Invoice downloads

### Marketing Tools:

#### 1. TopRank
- Premium placement in search results
- Increased brand visibility
- More orders from new and returning customers
- Factors normally affecting ranking: location, performance, customer reviews

#### 2. StampCards (Loyalty Programme)
- Customers earn stamps with each order
- Rewards loyal customers
- Attracts new customers
- **Key Stat:** 80% of turnover comes from 20% loyal customers
- Helps stand out from competition

#### 3. Order Now Toolkit
- Direct customers to restaurant's Lieferando page
- Integrates with restaurant's own marketing channels
- Boosts impact of current marketing efforts

#### 4. Platform Marketing Support
- Online and TV advertising by Lieferando
- Performance marketing (SEO optimization)
- Search engine visibility ("order pizza" → local pizzerias appear top)

### Commission Structure:
- **Commission-free:** Dine-in and pick-up orders via Takeaway Pay Cards
- **Standard Commissions:** Not publicly disclosed (contact Lieferando directly)
- **Affiliate Commissions:** Paid on order value excluding VAT, service charges, delivery charges, credit card fees

### Partner Benefits:
- Access to thousands of daily platform visitors
- Professional dashboard with business insights
- Marketing tools to increase visibility
- Loyalty programs to retain customers
- No tablet required with POS integration

## Lieferando Plus (Consumer Loyalty Program)
- Discount program for frequent customers
- Rewards at restaurants and retail partners
- Launch partners included Amazon
- Drives customer retention and repeat orders

## Market Dominance in Germany

### Current Statistics (2024-2025):
- **75% usage rate:** Among German consumers in past 12 months
- **Market share:** ~40% (post-merger with Lieferheld in 2019)
- **Global ranking:** #3830 globally, #1 in Germany Restaurants & Delivery
- **Monthly visitors:** 10+ million stable audience
- **Restaurant network:** 35,000+ partners

### Market Size & Growth:
- **2024 Market Size:** $13.5 billion USD (Germany online food delivery)
- **2025 Projected:** $19.81 billion USD
- **2033 Forecast:** $29.0 billion USD
- **CAGR:** 8.22-8.83% (2025-2033)
- **User Penetration:** 35.8% in 2025, expected to surpass 80% e-shopper penetration

### Competitive Landscape:
- **Primary Competitors:** Uber Eats, Wolt
- **Market Position:** Dominant player, but facing increased competition
- **Entry Barriers:** Global giants create barriers for regional startups
- **Competitive Advantages:**
  - First-mover advantage
  - Massive restaurant network
  - Strong brand recognition
  - Platform marketing investment

### Historical Consolidation:
- **2014:** Takeaway.com acquired Lieferando (€73M funding round)
- **2018:** Delivery Hero sold German subsidiaries to Takeaway.com
- **2019:** Lieferando-Lieferheld merger created 40% market dominance
- **2020:** Just Eat and Takeaway.com merged
- **2025:** Prosus acquired Just Eat Takeaway.com (€4.1B)

## Best Practices to Learn From

### 1. User Experience Excellence
- **Mobile-first approach:** 93% internet penetration, smartphone-first behavior
- **Seamless ordering:** Auto-location, saved addresses, one-tap reorder
- **Visual enhancements:** Bigger menu images, clear allergen info
- **Real-time tracking:** Food Tracker® with push notifications
- **Personalization:** Order history, favorites, recommendations

### 2. Restaurant Partner Empowerment
- **Comprehensive dashboard:** Single hub for all operations
- **Marketing tools included:** TopRank, StampCards, Order Now Toolkit
- **Data-driven insights:** Analytics section with KPIs
- **Flexibility:** Control hours, menu, pricing, availability
- **Commission-free options:** Takeaway Pay Cards for dine-in/pickup

### 3. Loyalty & Retention
- **StampCards:** Reward frequent customers
- **Lieferando Plus:** Platform-wide loyalty program
- **Partner benefits:** 80/20 rule - focus on loyal customer segment
- **Discounts & bundles:** German consumers expect value sensitivity

### 4. Market Expansion Strategy
- **Strategic acquisitions:** Lieferando + Lieferheld merger
- **Platform consolidation:** Unified Just Eat Takeaway brands
- **Local partnerships:** McDonald's, Spar express, local restaurants
- **Hybrid model:** Both own delivery (Scoober) and restaurant delivery

### 5. Technology & Integration
- **Open API:** Developer portal for POS integrations
- **Third-party friendly:** HubRise, MERGEPORT, Primasello support
- **Hybrid mode:** Flexibility for different restaurant needs
- **Performance marketing:** SEO optimization, search engine visibility

### 6. German Market Adaptation
- **Data privacy:** High sensitivity - SSL encryption emphasized
- **Sustainability focus:** Organic, locally sourced, plant-based options
- **Health consciousness:** Catering to younger demographics
- **Omnichannel approach:** Seamless web + app experience
- **Value proposition:** Discounts, loyalty perks, flash deals

### AVOID (What Not to Copy):

#### 1. Market Monopoly Tactics
- **Don't:** Acquire all competitors to dominate
- **Why:** Creates negative brand perception, regulatory scrutiny
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Collaborative ecosystem, coexist with competitors
- **Lieferando criticism:** 40% market share seen as monopolistic

#### 2. High Commission Structures
- **Don't:** Charge 20-30% commissions (Lieferando's likely rate)
- **Why:** Venues already have thin margins
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** 10-15% max, commission-free options
- **Industry note:** High commissions damage venue relationships

#### 3. Opaque Commission Disclosure
- **Don't:** Hide commission rates from public
- **Why:** Builds distrust, venues feel exploited
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Transparent pricing page
- **Transparency wins:** Builds long-term partnerships

#### 4. Tablet Dependency (Legacy Approach)
- **Don't:** Require dedicated hardware for venues
- **Why:** Friction, cost, training burden
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Web-based dashboard, mobile-responsive
- **Modern UX:** Cloud-based, no hardware needed

#### 5. Exclusive POS Lock-In
- **Don't:** Force exclusive POS integration
- **Why:** Limits venue flexibility
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Open API, multiple integration options
- **Lieferando issue:** POS API excludes tablet by default

#### 6. Minimal Social Features
- **Don't:** Treat platform as pure transaction tool
- **Why:** Misses opportunity for community building
- **WiesbadenAfterDark advantage:** Social-first design
- **Missed opportunity:** Lieferando lacks friend features, social discovery

#### 7. Generic National Approach
- **Don't:** One-size-fits-all platform
- **Why:** Loses local culture, personality
- **WiesbadenAfterDark strength:** Hyperlocal, Wiesbaden-focused
- **Differentiation:** Local expertise vs. national scale

#### 8. Platform-First Loyalty (Lieferando Plus)
- **Don't:** Make loyalty program about platform, not venues
- **Why:** Venues don't benefit directly
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Venue-specific loyalty + platform benefits
- **Example:** Venue stamp cards + WiesbadenAfterDark membership

#### 9. Aggressive Advertising Spend
- **Don't:** Require venues to pay for basic visibility
- **Why:** Small venues can't compete, creates pay-to-play
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Organic discovery + optional promotion
- **Fair model:** TopRank should be optional, not necessary

#### 10. Neglecting Restaurant/Venue Voice
- **Don't:** Make platform decisions without venue input
- **Why:** Venues feel like products, not partners
- **WiesbadenAfterDark approach:** Regular venue advisory board, feedback loops
- **Partnership model:** Co-create features with venues

## Key Takeaways for WiesbadenAfterDark

### Strategic Positioning:
- **Be the "Anti-Lieferando":** Transparent, fair, community-focused
- **Hyperlocal vs. National:** Deep Wiesbaden expertise vs. generic platform
- **Social-first:** Community building, not just transactions
- **Fair partnerships:** Lower commissions, transparent pricing, venue empowerment

### Technology Priorities:
1. Comprehensive venue dashboard (like Partner Hub)
2. Mobile-first PWA with offline support
3. Real-time tracking and notifications
4. Open API for integrations
5. Analytics and insights for venues

### Revenue Model:
- **Primary:** 10-15% commission on ticket sales
- **Secondary:** Featured placement (TopRank equivalent), premium memberships
- **Commission-free:** Direct reservations, table bookings
- **Transparency:** Public pricing page, clear fee breakdown

### Competitive Advantages:
1. **Local expertise** (Wiesbaden nightlife knowledge)
2. **Community features** (friends, groups, social discovery)
3. **Fair economics** (lower commissions than competitors)
4. **Experience focus** (not just transactions)
5. **Safety features** (unique to nightlife industry)
6. **Content support** (help venues market better)

- Monthly active users
- Visits per unique visitor
- Ticket purchase conversion rate
- Venue retention rate
- Customer lifetime value
- Platform NPS (Net Promoter Score)
- Venue satisfaction score
